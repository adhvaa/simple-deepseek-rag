Recovering from bone surgery requires a diet rich in specific nutrients to support healing and strengthen your bones. Here's a balanced diet plan to help you recover:

Daily Diet Plan
Breakfast
Oatmeal with Milk: Oatmeal is rich in fiber, and milk provides calcium and vitamin D.

Fresh Fruit: Add some berries or a sliced kiwi for vitamin C.

Nuts and Seeds: Sprinkle some almonds or chia seeds for added protein and healthy fats.

Mid-Morning Snack
Greek Yogurt: High in protein and calcium.

Orange Juice: Fortified with vitamin D and vitamin C.

Lunch
Grilled Chicken Salad: Chicken for protein, mixed greens for calcium and vitamin K, and a variety of colorful vegetables for vitamins and minerals.

Whole Grain Bread: Provides fiber and essential nutrients.

Afternoon Snack
Hummus with Carrot Sticks: Hummus is a good source of protein and healthy fats, while carrots provide vitamin A.

Dinner
Baked Salmon: Rich in omega-3 fatty acids and vitamin D.

Steamed Broccoli: High in calcium and vitamin C.

Quinoa: A great source of protein and all essential amino acids.

Evening Snack
Cottage Cheese with Pineapple: Cottage cheese is high in protein and calcium, and pineapple provides vitamin C.

Key Nutrients to Focus On
Protein: Essential for tissue repair and muscle strength. Include lean meats, fish, eggs, dairy products, nuts, seeds, and legumes.

Calcium: Vital for bone formation and strength. Found in dairy products, leafy greens, almonds, and fortified plant-based milk.

Vitamin D: Helps with calcium absorption. Sources include oily fish, egg yolks, fortified foods, and sunlight.

Vitamin C: Important for collagen production. Include citrus fruits, berries, tomatoes, and bell peppers.

Vitamin K: Strengthens bone protein and improves calcium absorption. Found in green leafy vegetables, Brussels sprouts, and broccoli.

Iron: Essential for collagen production and oxygen transport. Sources include red meat, dark poultry, oily fish, eggs, and fortified cereals.

Zinc: Promotes bone regeneration. Found in meat, seeds, legumes, nuts, and whole grains.

Potassium: Helps retain calcium in the body. Found in bananas, potatoes, oranges, and milk.
